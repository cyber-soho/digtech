2. The Three Kingdoms of ML

Machine Learning isn't one thing. It's broadly categorized into three main approaches based on how the learning happens.

üë®‚Äçüè´

Learning with a Teacher

The algorithm is trained on labeled data. It knows the "correct" answer for past examples and tries to predict answers for new data.

Goal

Predict labels for new data.

Examples

  • Spam Detection (Spam/Not Spam)
  • House Price Prediction
  • Medical Diagnosis
üß©

Finding Hidden Patterns

The algorithm is given data without explicit labels. It must discover the structure, clusters, or anomalies on its own, like a child sorting LEGOs by color.

Goal

Discover structure in data.

Examples

  • Customer Segmentation
  • Anomaly Detection
  • Recommendation Systems
üêï

Trial and Error

The agent interacts with an environment and learns through a system of rewards and penalties, seeking to maximize the long-term reward.

Goal

Learn optimal behavior strategy.

Examples

  • Self-driving Cars
  • Game Playing (Chess, Go)
  • Robotics

5. The Dark Side: Ethics & Responsibility

With great power comes great responsibility. ML models are not neutral; they reflect the data they are fed.

Bias & Fairness

"Garbage in, Bias out." If historical data contains discrimination (e.g., hiring bias), the model will learn and amplify it.

Alert: Amazon's hiring AI discriminated against resumes containing "Women's".

The Black Box

Deep learning models are often uninterpretable. In healthcare or criminal justice, not knowing why a decision was made is dangerous.

Challenge: Explainable AI (XAI) is now a critical field of research.

Environmental Cost

Training massive models consumes enormous energy. GPT-3 training emitted as much CO2 as 5 cars produce in their lifetime.

Stat: ~1,287 MWh for one training run.
